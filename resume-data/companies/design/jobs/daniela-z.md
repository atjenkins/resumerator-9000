# Interior Design Intern

## About the company
Boutique design studio providing bespoke, end-to-end solutions for a luxury clientele. Ready to grow and looking for the right person to grow with them.

## Role Overview
The Interior Design Intern will be involved in daily tasks such as assisting with space planning, selecting furniture, fixtures, and equipment (FF&E), and collaborating with the design team to develop interior design concepts. ## Responsibilities
- Help create presentations, mood boards, and client decks
- Work in Canva, SketchUp, CAD (or learn quickly)
- Assist with selections, samples, and sourcing
- Place orders and track them
- Help manage invoices, paperwork, and organization
- Support installations and project coordination
- Engage with clients and foster relationships
- Maintain the studio's resource library and keep it up-to-date
- Be part of both the creative and business processes
- Prepare design presentations
- Source materials
- Maintain project documentation

## Basic Qualifications
- Out of school or early in your career
- Eager, responsible, and self-motivated
- Interested in design as a long-term path
- Not afraid of details, deadlines, or learning by doing
- Organized, reliable, and proud of your work
- Comfortable wearing many hats
- Willing to learn anything
- Work ethic, responsibility, curiosity, and integrity

## Compensation & Benefits
- Hourly position with a commitment of 20-30 hours per week
- Compensation based on experience and potential
- As skills, confidence, and contribution to the studio grow, so will role and compensation
- Designed as a long-term opportunity with room to grow into a designer, project manager, or leader in the studio

## Additional Details
This is not a short-term position - looking for someone who sees a future with the studio and wants to build something real. Some days are beautiful and creative, some days are detailed and administrative - all days matter. Opportunity to learn how real design projects are built from start to finish, how to work with clients, vendors, and contractors, and how a design business actually functions.
